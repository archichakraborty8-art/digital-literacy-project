For this task, I used Canva because of its intuitive drag-and-drop interface and professional templates. My infographic focuses on defining digital literacy, highlighting essential tools for B.Tech students like GitHub and Kaggle, and providing a quick checklist for safe internet practices. I aimed for a minimalist design to ensure the information is easily digestible for my peers.
One interesting thing I found while making this was how broad digital literacy actually is; it isn’t just about knowing how to use a computer, but about critical thinking in a digital space. The most difficult part was condensing complex safety protocols into short, actionable bullet points without losing the core message. This exercise taught me that as a Digital Ambassador, visual communication is just as important as technical knowledge when it comes to peer outreach.
